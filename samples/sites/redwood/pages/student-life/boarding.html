Boarding Life

A warm, purposeful home at the heart of the school

Boarding at Redwood Science Academy is not merely an arrangement of logistical convenience — it is a deliberate educational experience in its own right. When pupils live alongside one another and are genuinely cared for by house parents who know them as individuals, the depth of learning — academic and personal alike — accelerates in ways that simply cannot be replicated in a day school.

Our four boarding houses each accommodate between forty and fifty pupils from Years 7 to 13. Each house has its own identity, traditions and culture, yet all share the same commitment: that every boarder feels genuinely at home, genuinely supported and genuinely known. Our boarding welfare team maintains regular contact with families throughout the term, and our pastoral record is among the strongest in the independent sector.

What We Offer

Four cornerstones of an exceptional boarding experience

Full & Weekly Boarding

Families choose between full boarding — in which pupils remain on campus throughout the term — and weekly boarding, with a return home each weekend. Both options carry identical access to pastoral support, co-curricular activities and the full warmth of house life.

House System

Redwood, Stirling, Langdale and Cairn — our four houses each carry a distinct character shaped over decades by their pupils and their house parents. Inter-house competition in sport, music, debating and the arts fosters a powerful sense of belonging and a healthy spirit of friendly rivalry.

Pastoral Care

Every boarder is assigned a personal tutor and has ready access to our resident counsellor, school nurse and chaplaincy team. Weekly wellbeing check-ins, an anonymous reporting system and regular, open communication with families ensure that no pupil ever struggles in silence.

Weekend Programme

Weekends at Redwood are never empty. Cultural visits to London, Edinburgh and beyond sit alongside on-campus activities: film nights, cookery sessions, arts workshops, sporting fixtures and the precious freedom to simply explore the campus in the company of friends.

House Life

Where friendships are forged for a lifetime

Each boarding house has its own distinct character: a common room that feels genuinely like a family sitting room, quiet study spaces equipped with high-speed broadband, a full kitchen available to pupils in the evenings, and a house library stocked with everything from graphic novels to classical literature. House parents live on site and are present each morning, every evening and throughout the night.

The friendships formed in the boarding houses are among the most enduring of our alumni's lives. Pupils from more than thirty-two nations share these spaces, and the daily exchange of cultures, languages and perspectives constitutes an education in itself. Our boarding community is one of the most genuinely international aspects of life at Redwood, and we treasure it deeply.

The Daily Rhythm

A day in the life of a Redwood boarder

Structure and freedom are balanced carefully across every day. There is always enough routine to feel secure and enough space to feel genuinely free.

Morning

Boarders rise at 07:00 and enjoy a full cooked or continental breakfast in the dining hall between 07:15 and 08:00. A brief house meeting — always warm in tone and occasionally quite funny — sets the spirit for the day before pupils make their way to their first lesson of the morning.

Lessons

The academic day runs from 08:30 to 15:30 with a one-hour lunch break. Boarders have access to the library, science preparation rooms and music practice studios throughout the day, and many choose to attend optional study periods with subject teachers during free periods.

Activities & Sport

From 15:45 to 18:00, the whole school comes alive with co-curricular activity. Boarders participate in sport, music rehearsals, drama, the Duke of Edinburgh's Award programme and a rich menu of clubs and societies. No pupil is ever at a loose end during this time at Redwood.

House Supper

From 18:30, house supper in the dining hall is a genuinely social occasion and one of the anchors of the boarding day. House tables deliberately mix year groups, encouraging older pupils to take responsibility for welcoming younger members into the community. Conversation and laughter are warmly encouraged by house parents.

Prep & Rest

Evening prep runs from 19:30, supervised in house study rooms where a house tutor circulates and remains on hand for academic support. Younger pupils have lights-out at 21:00; seniors manage their own timetable within agreed boundaries. Every boarder closes the day with a brief pastoral check-in from their house parent — an unhurried conversation that matters far more than it might initially appear.
